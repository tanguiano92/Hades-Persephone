Hades x Persephone

Artist's Statement



Artist Statement




I created “HadesxPersephone” as a therapy piece. As we know, these last few months have been extremely difficult
for everyone. However, within the past two years, I have lost thirteen family members and now with the pandemic
causing a rift between my ability to visit family, depression and harsh thoughts have been creeping into my psyche.
While in quarantine, however, I found myself becoming more and more interested in classic Greek mythos and connecting
specific stories to my own intrigues. The connection between Life and Death has always peaked my curiousity because both
aspects are always thrown up in the air with theories and questions. No one has definitive answers for why we are here
or where we go when we are gone. Theologies try to make sense of it through sermons and beliefs, while scienctists scratch
their heads in uncertanty. Plain and simple, we are just here. Going through the same cycle of living and dying but no two
lives are lived the same way. This thought always reminds me of the tale of Hades and Persephone; essentially the king of
the underworld and the fertility/nature Goddess coming together and becoming married prompted me to wonder about the so-called
divide between the realms of life and death. Are they truly divided? Or are they interconnected in a way that we do not
think about, or want to? Why are people so scared of the one constant we all have in common? In my studies during the making
of this small, but meaningful piece, the answer became clear: they go hand in hand in a harmonious, loving relationship. So
often, we are made to feel that we should be afraid of death and that it is such a taboo thing when in reality, we will all
meet the same fate. Death teaches us to live and live well within the way we want to. Don't waste time worrying about mundane
things. I created this piece as a reminder that life is a beautiful gift and the inevitable death that awaits me in the long
off future is not something to be feared, but just another adventure into the mysterious unknown once I'm done here.
